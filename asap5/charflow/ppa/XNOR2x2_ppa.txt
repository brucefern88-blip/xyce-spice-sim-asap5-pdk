Notes:
- tpd = (|cell_rise| + |cell_fall|) / 2
- Topology: XOR TG core (nfin=4) drives internal node X, output INV (nfin=4) to Y
- B=0: TG2 passes ~A to X, inverter gives Y=A (non-inverting overall)
- nfin=4 output inverter gives very sharp transitions (~10ps @ 0.7V)
- Energy ~1.4x higher than XNOR2x1 due to doubled fin count (more gate cap)
- 2x drive: 12% faster tpd than XNOR2x1 (58.9 vs 67.1 ps @ 0.7V)
- Energy measured over one full transition cycle (1400 ps period)
- 0.40V SLVT achieves 1.16x energy reduction at 2.94x delay cost
- EDP worse at 0.40V (211.4 vs 83.0 aJ*ps) — not favorable for near-threshold

Comparison with XNOR2x1 (same area):
  tpd:    58.9 vs 67.1 ps @ 0.7V  (12% faster, nfin=4 vs nfin=2)
  energy: 1.409 vs 0.997 fJ       (41% more energy)
  EDP:    83.0 vs 66.9 aJ*ps      (24% worse EDP — speed gain < energy cost)
